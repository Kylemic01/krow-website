How this is calculated

This estimate starts from your annual SDE (seller's discretionary earnings — your real economic take-home as the owner, not just reported profit) and applies a multiple based on the factors that most directly move what HVAC buyers actually pay. The data is drawn from 2026 HVAC M&A transaction activity, BizBuySell deal data, and practitioner ranges from active HVAC M&A advisors.

For small HVAC companies (under $1M SDE), buyers typically use SDE-based multiples rather than EBITDA, because you as the owner-operator are part of the economics. The SDE multiple range for this tier is 2.0x–4.5x in 2026, with where you land in that range driven almost entirely by the factors below.

The single biggest lever in HVAC valuation is your service/maintenance agreement base. Buyers treat maintenance contracts as an underwriteable, recurring asset — businesses with 50%+ service agreement revenue routinely command 0.5x–1.0x higher multiples than installation-heavy operations, and a strong maintenance base can add 2x–3x its annual value to your total purchase price. This one factor can be worth hundreds of thousands of dollars in exit value.
